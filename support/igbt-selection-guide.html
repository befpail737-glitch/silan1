1. Voltage Rating Selection

The first and most critical parameter in IGBT selection is the voltage rating. Selecting an IGBT with insufficient voltage rating can lead to catastrophic failure, while over-specifying increases cost unnecessarily.

Voltage Rating Guidelines

Application DC Bus Voltage Recommended VCES Silan IGBT Series
230V AC Motor Drive 310-400V DC 600V SGT40N60NPF, SGT50N65
400V AC Industrial 540-650V DC 1200V SDM30N120, SDM50N120
Solar Inverter ( residential) 350-450V DC 600V SGT30N60, SGT40N60NPF
Welding Machine 300-400V DC 600V SGT40N60NPF, SGT50N65

💡 Design Tip

Always include a safety margin of 1.5x to 2x between your maximum operating voltage and the IGBT's VCES rating. This accounts for voltage spikes, switching transients, and derating at high temperatures.

2. Current Rating Selection

The current rating determines how much power your IGBT can handle. It's crucial to calculate both continuous and peak current requirements accurately.

Current Calculation Steps

  1. Calculate RMS Current: Determine the RMS current based on your load profile and duty cycle.
  2. Apply Safety Factor: Multiply RMS current by 1.5 to 2.0 for reliable operation.
  3. Consider Overload: Account for startup currents and temporary overloads (typically 2x rated current for 1 minute).
  4. Temperature Derating: IGBT current rating decreases at high temperatures. Check the datasheet curves.

Current Rating Quick Reference

3. Switching Speed Requirements

Switching speed affects both efficiency and EMI. Faster switching reduces switching losses but increases EMI and voltage spikes.

Switching Frequency Guidelines

Low Frequency (1-5 kHz)

  • Motor drives (>5kW)
  • Welding equipment
  • Induction heating

Recommendation: Standard speed IGBTs (e.g., SGT40N60)

Medium Frequency (5-20 kHz)

  • UPS and inverters
  • Small motor drives
  • Power supplies

Recommendation: Fast switching IGBTs (e.g., SGT40N60NPF)

High Frequency (>20 kHz)

  • SMPS
  • DC-DC converters
  • Induction cookers

Recommendation: Ultra-fast IGBTs or consider MOSFETs

4. Thermal Management

Proper thermal management is essential for reliable IGBT operation. The junction temperature must be kept below Tj(max) under all operating conditions.

Thermal Calculation

Tj = Ta + Ptotal × (RthJC + RthCS + RthSA)

  • Tj = Junction temperature (°C)
  • Ta = Ambient temperature (°C)
  • Ptotal = Total power dissipation (W)
  • RthJC = Junction-to-case thermal resistance (°C/W)
  • RthCS = Case-to-heatsink thermal resistance (°C/W)
  • RthSA = Heatsink-to-ambient thermal resistance (°C/W)

6. IGBT vs MOSFET Selection

Choosing between IGBT and MOSFET depends on voltage, current, and switching frequency requirements.

Parameter IGBT MOSFET
Voltage Range 600V - 6500V Up to 1000V (optimal <200V)
Current Range 10A - 1000A+ Up to 200A (optimal <50A)
Switching Frequency 1-50 kHz (optimal <20kHz) Up to 1 MHz (optimal >100kHz)
Conduction Loss (High Current) Lower Higher
Switching Loss Higher Lower
Cost (High Voltage) Lower Higher

Quick Decision Guide

  • Voltage > 400V AND Current > 10A: Use IGBT
  • Frequency > 50kHz: Use MOSFET
  • Voltage < 200V AND Current < 20A: Use MOSFET
  • 400-600V, 10-50A, <20kHz: Either - compare losses

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I choose the right IGBT for my application?

To choose the right IGBT, consider four key factors: 1) Voltage rating - select VCES at least 1.5-2x your DC bus voltage, 2) Current rating - choose IC based on RMS current with 1.5-2x safety margin, 3) Switching speed - faster for high frequency (>20kHz), slower for better EMI, 4) Thermal performance - ensure RthJC allows safe operation at max temperature.

What is the difference between IGBT and MOSFET?

IGBTs combine MOSFET input characteristics with bipolar output, making them ideal for high-voltage (>300V), high-current applications. MOSFETs are better for lower voltage, high-frequency switching. IGBTs have lower conduction losses at high currents but slower switching speeds compared to MOSFETs.

What voltage rating IGBT do I need?

Select an IGBT voltage rating (VCES) at least 1.5 to 2 times your maximum DC bus voltage. For 400V DC bus (rectified 230VAC), use 600V IGBTs. For 600V DC bus, use 1200V IGBTs. This provides safety margin for voltage spikes and transients.
